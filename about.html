About

Summary

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Melbourne involved in integrating proteomics and systems genetics research. I am focused on developing and implementing novel bioinformatics approaches to improve or automate analytical workflows.

Experience

Computational biologist - The University of Melbourne In my current role I'm responsible for all data science and software development in the Metabolic Proteomics & Signal Transduction group. Reproducible and automated data science workflows are devloped using expertise in cloud computing and high performance computing (HPC). I led multiple software development projects resulting in hosted online webservers for the analysis of big data, as well as scientific publications in prominent journals.

Research officer - QIMR Berghofer My roles in the Precision & Systems Biomedicine group varied from machine learning, workflow development, stakeholder engagement and teaching. Machine learning and in-house developed workflows were used to study various diseases to identify potential novel interventions and therapies. Structured and unstructured datasets were mined to extract findings driving further experiments. A significant portion of my role included consulting on projects from internal and external projects, providing input in experimental design, data acquisition, analysis and reporting. I provided workshops and presentations within the institute and at conferences.

PhD Candidate - The University of Queensland As a PhD candidate at the University of Queensland I employed statistical and machine learning approaches to classify patient biopsies and discover the biological features of esophageal cancer. By combining patient database records with mass spectrometry measurements I identified and validated the role of lipid metabolism in the disease progression. During my employment, I took the opportunity to receive training and collaborate at the National University of Singapore and Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam in experimental design, data analysis and machine learning.
